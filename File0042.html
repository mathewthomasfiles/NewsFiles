Introduction[edit]

Aadhaar has[1] been claimed to be the most reliable ID in the country with an argument that unlike other IDs it cannot be faked easily. Although the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI)[1] may have explained the convenience due to Aadhaar[2], the fundamental question still remains whether Aadhaar is as reliable and infallible as it is claimed to be.

The following article is a visualization of the cases of fraud based on aadhaar. You can sort the date and get a timeline of all fraudulent crimes[3] in which aadhaar is a tool. Supreme Court of India did not make aadhaar mandatory for any of the subsidies or daily provisions. It is no exaggeration that citizens of India face a lot of inconvenience because aadhaar is mandatory and the verification tools are poor and the provisions were denied. There are numerous cases since 2012 where citizens were denied basic provisions, and this is sometimes fatal. Aadhaar poses serious threat to human rights.One is Right to life because necessary provisions and supplies are being denied for a citizen on basis of aadhaar or failure in authentication of aadhaar. Second is Right to privacy because the bio-metric and personal data of millions of Indians are compromised. Following are the details of it discussed briefly.

Summary[edit]

It is clear that all the frauds have Aadhaar in common. There is a clear abuse in usage of Aadhaar and UIDAI[101] has not taken any measures to close the leak. Instead UIDAI pretends there is no leak. Also, claims that the privacy standards are set too high besides having millions of Indian citizens privacy at risk.Tathagata Satpathy[102], representing Dhenkanal constituency of Odisha) also said that Aadhaar can be a tool for ethnic cleansing in future.

This article is not to be mistaken as an outrage but a source to identify the failures of Aadhaar system.
